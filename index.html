Satoru Iwata

The Legacy of the former Nintendo President

  • 1959 - Born in Sapporo, Japan
  • 1978 - Admitted into the Tokyo Institute of Technology for Computer Science, while working an unpaid internship at Commodore Japan
  • 1980 -While attending university, Iwata works for HAL Laboratory part-time. Among the first creations was a peripheral device that enabled older computesr to display graphics for video games.
  • 1982 - After graduating, works full-time for HAL Laboratory as it's only programmer
  • 1983 - Becomes the comapny's coordiator of software production, helps create a relationship with Nintendo. Works on games such as Joust, Balloon Fight, NES Open Tournament Golf, EarthBound, and the Kirby games.
  • 1993 - Iwata is promoted to President of HAL after gaining high esteem from fellow programmers and gamers for his proficiency with programming
  • 2000 - Takes a position at Nintendo as the head of the corportate planning division and a seat on the board of directors. The next two years he sought to reduce the cost and length of game development while preserving quality. Nintendo sees profit increases of 20 and 41 percent
  • 2002 - Succeeds Hiroshi Yamauchi as President of Nintendo, at Yamauchi's blessing.
  • 2004 - Iwata implements a "blue ocean" strategy, focusing on producing novel and entertaining hardware and games. The Nintendo DS is released this year.
  • 2006 - The Nintendo Wii is released, popularizing motion-controlled based games. Under his leadership, the Wii helped nearly double Nintendo's stocks.
  • 2011-2014 - After the decline of sales due to the Nintendo 3DS and Wii U, Iwata voluntarily halves his salary as apologies for the poor sales, preventing lay-offs in the company.
  • 2013 - Iwata takes the additional role of Nintendo of America's CEO
  • 2014 - Creates physical toys "Amiibo" to connect to games, becomes a huge success.
  • 2015 - Iwata oversees development of the Nintendo Switch through the final months of his life as the console's head developer. The Nintendo Switch later becomes a great success for Nintendo
  • 2015 - On July 11 at the age 55, Satoru Iwata dies due to complications of a tumor discovered in his bile duct.
